OVERVIEW

Introduction

The City of Surrey has opened a project to construct Bolivar Park Secondary, a new secondary school that will be on the current land of Bolivar Park, Surrey, BC. It will be the only major secondary school in the area, and will provide high-quality, accessible education for this large part of North Surrey. As one of the few major secondary school projects presented to the City of Surrey as of late, various building proposals and site selections have been made. A detailed analysis of the two major building proposals and the final conclusion are shown on this website.

Site A: Bolivar Park

Highlighted in red, this potential site for a secondary school has been conclusively selected to be the construction site. Bolivar Park Secondary, in the below site, has shown to be the most promising proposal.

Site B: 168 Street & 20th Ave Area

The second major site, the proposed areas are shown highlighted in red. Despite showing other promising qualities, ultimately, Bolivar Park was chosen over this site from our analysis. The report is linked among the other tabs.

GEOGRAPHY

Geographical analysis

The City of Surrey has conducted an analysis of the geographical factors concerning the two proposed sites.

Site A (Bolivar Park) Details

  • Address: 13591 Crestview Drive, Surrey BC
  • Large area with many different activity sites including multiple baseball fields, a field house and washroom building, playground, and many picnic tables
  • In the south and east part of the park there is a large forested area with multiple forest trails
  • Some of these areas offer highly esteemed views of New Westminster and the Fraser River
  • Includes a two-acre fenced dog off-leash area - mostly open field grass

Bolivar Park is an extremely large area with a large amount of empty space, and large swaths of forested plains. Construction sites are optimal in many of these areas geographically.

Site B (168 Street & 20th Ave Area) Details

  • This intersection is unique in that this is the only intersection of two roads in the surrounding area
  • This area is rather sparse in terms of roads
  • Mostly residential housing, with large farmland plains in between, suitable for construction
  • Streets criss-cross to form an extremely large plain in the middle

This specific area surrounding the intersection of 168 Street & 20th Ave is mostly residential area. However, there are large plains that are suitable for construction, so there are no major issues in this factor.

Conclusion

Both sites feature suitable areas for construction. Geographically, both sites are also fairly well-suited for demolition and the following processes, so no large concerns arise here.

ENVIRONMENT

Site A (Bolivar Park) Details

Cutting a large amount of area out for a school will significantly impact the environment surrounding Bolivar Park. Since each park site is interconnected tightly, many areas offering a combination of two different sites, building a school in this site will completely remove large swaths of multiple sites. The only open grasslands are the dog park and the large forested area. The forested area will have to be cut down in order to build a school here, which will significantly impact the views of the surrounding environment and cause damage to the forest and nearby nature. Many of the park's amenities are connected to each other through the large swath of open land that is the dog park, so interfering with the dog park will negatively disrupt the environment surrounding the other sites as well.

Site B (168 Street & 20th Ave Area) Details

For this site, it will significantly disrupt the amount of trees and nature there. The demolition of the natural fields surrounding the area will impact the environment. However, for all secondary schools and construction projects in general, a positive is that secondary schools can ensure that the surrounding area is taken care of (parks, fields, trees, etc.)

Conclusion

Many of the environment risks, hazards, and damages are standard to all construction projects, and many of these effects are temporary and minimal, having that environmental damages are shown to be minimized over a long-term period. The City of Surrey stands by its commitment to decrease the environment risks, hazards, and damages for all of its construction projects, and its plan for Bolivar Park Secondary is outlined below:

Reduce Noise

Surrey recognizes that construction noise is a major source of noise pollution. Most of this is produced by machinery in site preparation, demolition, and landscaping. In residential areas, construction sites can often cause complaints or reports from nearby homes. When beginning the Bolivar Park Secondary project, Surrey commits to adhering to all local construction time restrictions. The City of Surrey will also publish a schedule and noise report for those affected.

Efficient Waste Disposal

In Canada, demolition waste makes up 90% of total debris, and this waste is disposed of in landfills or through incineration. The construction project commits to salvaging, reusing and recycling existing materials,cutting down on materials harming the environment Additionally, Hardware, appliances, and fixtures will be largely recycled or reused.

Renewable Technology Innovation

The City of Surrey uses inflatable water dams, which help combat erosion, water runoff, and prevents sedimentation. Sandbags and traditional dewatering solutions have proven to be costly and time-consuming. New inflatable water dams are reusable and take up little space, and are easier to install and environmentally friendly.

The City of Surrey also has expediting construction completion and preperation, which will help speed the process.

ECONOMY

Cnstructing a school on a large, open land will be the most economically beneficial plan. In the site of Bolivar Park, Aside from the open swaths of dog park or the large forested area, there are the large sites such as the baseball fields or the other amenities. These will cost significantly as the demolition costs will be higher (existing equipment). The City of Surrey already owns the rights to the park land, so demolition will not have huge impacts on the cost or legal costs. However, cutting out a large site on the intersection of 168 and 20th street will require a lot of buying up properties, a large economic impact. Additionally, secondary school maintenance costs are significant, including water, electricity, salary payment, renovation costs, etc. Citizen livelihoods also depend on the location of their home, so this will be affected if the government pressures people in selling their home, only later to realize it was an undesirable decision. Because of the above factors, an empty construction site will almost certainly be preferred over building over existing properties. Adding to the economic impact, a school built in any location will attract a huge number of jobs and economic opportunities for the businesses nearby. The economical impacts are also influenced by how many people live surrounding the chosen site. Although more people live near Site B, the existence of another secondary school near this site, Grandview Heightse Secondary, reduces the economic potential. Overall, the negative economic impacts of school construction is outweighed by the economic opportunities and employment presented by a large secondary school. The maintainance costs are balanced by the overall positive growth from employment and contracts.

POLITICAL

Building this secondary school will mean more education, higher community satisfaction. There are different political factors for each of the two proposed sites.

Site A (Bolivar Park) Details

The government will have to get a building permit in or near the park in order to construct the school and move on to the other processes. Since the government already has many of the rights/permits required for the park, this factor will be much easier to streamline and go through. However, the construction process itself will be more difficult to go through than in a residential area with free space, as significant permits will be required in order to excavate and construct the already existing park space. The government will have to provide an alternative space to important facilities and areas, such as the dog park or the washrooms nearby.

Site B (168 Street & 20th Ave Area) Details

The field is extremely easy to get a permit for, as empty spaces are usually simpler than already existing properties to construct on. Other processes follow as above.

The BC Regional Services building permit process currently is as follows:

Building Permits may be issued when:

  • The proposed work substantially conforms with the current building code, building bylaw and any other applicable bylaws or zoning regulations
  • All the fees are paid
  • The owner has retained a professional engineer if required
  • The owner has retained an architect if required
  • The owner has retained a BCLS survey if required
  • The owner has been granted DP/DVP/BOV if required

After all of these processes have been followed, other political factors are less immediate.

SOCIAL

Site B (168 Street & 20th Ave Area) Details

One of the most important factors in school construction is placement; specifically, where to maximize the amount of reach. This is extremely important as school constructions are a tedious and expensive process, so we want to maximize the output from these schools. There are no open fields extremely close to the intersection of 168 and 20th street - the nearest one is pictured below.

Note how Grandview Heights Secondary School is only a few blocks away from this optimal site. This makes this site almost immediately not available for consideration as two extremely close by secondary schools are redundant, especially considering the quality and expenses of Grandview Secondary. Although this site is not the only proposed construction site, it is far too close to another high-quality secondary school to consider building such a large-scale project. This social factor makes this zone a suboptimal place to construct.

Site A (Bolivar Park) Details

On the contrary, take a look at Bolivar Park and its surrounding area. There are no secondary schools in the area. This shows that the money and construction invested in the project would best benefit this area, of which its large population is not best served without a secondary school. Other than the specific site benefits, secondary schools are an essential part of a growing community. Surrey's community is growing faster than ever, so a secondary school would benefit the overall community happiness, education, and social services. Overall, if constructed, managed, and maintained properly, the economic and political downsides would be outweighed by the strong social and some physical factors.

CONCLUSION

The City of Surrey's construction of Bolivar Park Secondary has yet to have a start date, as the proposals have just been finalized. However, our analysis of the two major proposals has brought a clear choice, of which that is Bolivar Park. A combination of all of the above factors have led to this decision. All five factors are listed below.

Geography

Geographically, both sites were deemed to be in good condition for construction. However, slightly more choice was given to Bolivar Park, as there is more empty forestry to construct on.

Environment

Ignoring which site is chosen, the construction process will have an environmental impact customary for every construction. The City of Surrey has outlined a plan to minimize the environmental risks, as shown in the "Environment" tab.

Economy

This factor is independent of which site is chosen, but Bolivar Park is more beneficial towards the economy as a site. This is because attendance rates will be higher, because of the lack of another secondary school around the site.

Political

Building permits will be easier to obtain at Bolivar Park, because of the inherent government rights to the land. Attached in the corresponding tab is the set of steps in order to obtain a permit, for reference.

Social

Although most factors leading up to this have been fairly close, ultimately, the deciding factor is Social. The simple existence of another high-quality secondary school near Site B means that the construction effort and money would be of a lesser impact compared to building a secondary school in a district where there is not another. Thus, Bolivar Park is the clear choice for the social factor.
